Infrared universe

The first photo rleased by the James Webb Telescope offered a view of the deep universe taken in an Infrared image. We can see thousands of galaxies from this photo alone. The size of this image is equivelant to the size of a grain of sand held at arm's legnth by someone on the ground.

A dying star

This dying star can be seen expelling gas and dust in all directions. With this view we are able to get a finely detailed look at the Southern Ring planetary nebula that was previously hidden from astronomers. In the background we see many galaxies spread throughout.

Cosmic Cliffs

The infrared technology of the James Webb Telescope offers a chance for us to learn more about stars and how they form by peering through the dust. The valley and mountain-like structures here are a star-forming region in the Carina Nebula.

Stephan's Quintet

Revealed in stunning detail we can see these galaxies interacting with each other, forming new stars. This is one of James Webb Telescopes largest images yet, cotaining over 150 million pixels, as well as being composed of 1,000 seperate image files.
